Teacher Support Resources


We recommend that some preparatory work be done with the children before viewing the Story of the Universe film. The activities suggested are intended to awaken curiosity and a readiness to take in the contents of the film. These exercises are in the form of Questions and Stories.

Likewise we include activities to be undertaken post viewing the film. It is hoped that the activities offered will facilitate the children to continue to explore the story and its implications in more depth. To re-enact the Universe Story as simple drama is one option. There are also some stories and exercises which explore the themes of the interconnectedness and interdependence and our kinship with other species everything.

SUGGESTED PREPARATORY LESSONS:

Activities in preparation for viewing the film.

⍟ QUESTIONS

⭒ 1.THE SEED: This exercise is designed to facilitate the children to raise questions. It is important not to give answers but to allow the film at a later stage to give the answers. You will need an acorn or a picture of one.

Take the Acorn. Examine it. What is it? Where did it come from? Where did the oak tree come from? Where did the acorn that grew that tree come from? This leads to an infinite number of questions in order to get to the first seed. Leave the children with the question of where the original acorn came from. The film will supply that answer.

(DIAGRAM of the acorn– oak –acorn – oak– acorn – oak etc etc.)

⭒ 2. THE UNIVERSE: The objective is to get the children, unprompted, to respond to the word ‘Universe’. Sometimes the word Universe conjures up an image limited to stars, galaxies, space etc. Take their responses without comment.

This exercise can be repeated after viewing the film and observe how the children’s understanding of the word ‘Universe’ will have expanded.

SUGGESTED FOLLOW – UP LESSONS

Activities following viewing of the film.

⍟ UNIVERSE STORY - DRAMA

A simple dramatising of the Universe Story as a follow-up activity to the film is an enjoyable and creative way of facilitating the children to integrate the learnings into their personal lives. This can be done very simply. Children impersonate the various stages and characters of the story. They can be invited to draw or paint a picture of who they are impersonating. They can pin it on or string it around their necks. They can use the minimum script given here and encouraged to research and find out more about their character.
